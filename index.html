Major PETA Updates

Before moving forward to your final output, please take note of the following crucial updates regarding the grading system:

  • Equation Format: We are strictly adopting the format $\hat{y} = mx + c$ for our linear equation.
  • No Peer Evaluation: The peer evaluation component has been completely removed.
  • Total PETA Score: The final output is exactly 50 Points.

The 50 Points Breakdown:

  • Group Score (30 Points): Based strictly on the mathematical Calculations & Data Analysis part of your paper. Everyone in the group is responsible for ensuring calculations are 100% correct.
  • Individual Score (20 Points): Based exclusively on the specific written section of the paper you are assigned to complete.

Division of Labor (Individual Assignments)

To determine your 20-point Individual Score, group members must divide the written sections. You must declare who is doing what part on your title page.

Paper Section # of Students Primary Responsibility
Methodology Exactly 1 Write the sampling design, Green's rule calculation, and data variable definitions.
Discussion & Conclusion Exactly 2 Interpret the slope, discuss confounding variables (Correlation $\neq$ Causation), write conclusion & limitations.
Introduction Rest of Group Write context, objectives, and research question.

Crucial Rule: The number of cited research papers must be $\ge$ the number of students working on this section.

Mini-Research Paper Structure (Part 1)

1. Introduction (Individual Graded)

  • Provide background information/context about the real-world scenario.
  • State the primary objective and the specific research question (e.g., "Is there a significant relationship between $x$ and $y$?").
  • Ensure citations equal or exceed the number of students assigned here.

2. Methodology (Individual Graded)

  • Sampling Design: Identify the target population and sampling method.
  • Sample Size Calculation: Show Green's Rule:
    $$ N \ge 50 + 8k $$
    (Where $k=1$ predictor variable).
  • Data Variables: Describe $x$ and $y$ clearly with their exact units.

Mini-Research Paper Structure (Part 2)

3. Results & Analysis (Group Graded - 30 Pts)

  • Present Mean ($\bar{x}, \bar{y}$) and Standard Deviation ($s_x, s_y$).
  • Present Pearson's $r$ (strength and direction).
  • Include Scatter Plot with overlaid Regression Line.
  • Present slope ($m$) and y-intercept ($c$): $\hat{y} = mx + c$.
  • Provide a realistic prediction example using a theoretical $x$.

4. Discussion & Conclusion (Individual Graded)

  • Interpret the slope in context.
  • Correlation $\neq$ Causation: Explain why. Identify confounding variables.
  • Summarize findings and acknowledge limitations. Include APA References section at the very end.

Group Score Rubric: Data Analysis (30 Points)

Everyone is responsible for this section. If there are errors here, the whole group loses points.

Component Excellent (9–10 pts) Good (6–8 pts) Fair / Needs Imp. (1–5 pts)
Slope ($m$) & Y-intercept ($c$) Correct formulas used. Complete step-by-step solution provided, and final values perfectly accurate. Formulas are correct, but there is a minor calculation/rounding error in slope or intercept. Major computational errors, wrong formula used, or step-by-step missing.
Regression Equation & Prediction Equation correctly written as $\hat{y} = mx + c$. A logical $x$ is chosen, substituted correctly, and prediction is accurate with units. Equation formatted correctly, but prediction calculation contains minor errors or lacks units. Equation is not constructed properly, or prediction logic is completely incorrect.
Scatter Plot & Line Overlay Perfectly constructed with title/labels. Regression line accurately drawn over data points. Graph present but lacks title/labels, or the overlaid line is slightly skewed. Poorly constructed, points don't match data, or line missing.

Individual Rubric: Introduction (20 Points)

This rubric applies ONLY to the student(s) assigned to the Introduction section.

Criteria Excellent Good Fair / Needs Improvement
Context & Background
(8 pts)		 (7–8 pts) Highly engaging context that thoroughly explains the real-world scenario. (4–6 pts) Good context, but slightly vague or missing some background details. (1–3 pts) Vague, disorganized, or completely missing context.
Objectives & Research Question
(6 pts)		 (5–6 pts) Primary objective is crystal clear. Specific research question is formatted perfectly. (3–4 pts) Objective is stated, but the research question lacks specificity. (1–2 pts) Missing objective or poorly phrased research question.
Citations & APA Format
(6 pts)		 (5–6 pts) Mentions the exact required number of sources ($\ge$ assigned students). Properly cited in APA. (3–4 pts) Meets citation count but has formatting errors, OR is short by exactly 1 citation. (1–2 pts) Missing citations completely or severely under the required amount.

Individual Rubric: Methodology (20 Points)

This rubric applies ONLY to the single student assigned to the Methodology section.

Criteria Excellent Good Fair / Needs Improvement
Sampling Design
(8 pts)		 (7–8 pts) Clearly and accurately defines both the target population and the specific sampling method chosen. (4–6 pts) Mentions population and method, but the description lacks detail or clarity. (1–3 pts) Missing target population or fails to identify the sampling method.
Sample Size Calculation
(6 pts)		 (5–6 pts) Explicitly shows Green's Rule ($N \ge 50 + 8k$) step-by-step with the correct final required size. (3–4 pts) Uses Green's rule but calculation is not fully explicit, or has a minor arithmetic error. (1–2 pts) Does not justify sample size or completely misapplies the rule.
Data Variables
(6 pts)		 (5–6 pts) Variables ($x$ and $y$) are perfectly defined, clearly stating their specific units of measurement. (3–4 pts) Variables are defined but missing their exact units or measurement method. (1–2 pts) Variables are poorly defined, swapped, or entirely missing.

Individual Rubric: Discussion & Conclusion (20 Pts)

This rubric applies to the two students assigned here. They share this 20-point grade.

Criteria Excellent Good Fair / Needs Improvement
Slope Interpretation
(7 pts)		 (6–7 pts) Perfect, logical interpretation of what the slope means in the real-world context of the variables. (4–5 pts) Interpretation is mostly correct but wording is slightly confusing or lacks context. (1–3 pts) Fails to interpret the slope, or interpretation is mathematically incorrect.
Causation & Confounders
(7 pts)		 (6–7 pts) Explicitly explains correlation $\neq$ causation. Intelligently identifies and explains confounding variables. (4–5 pts) Mentions confounding variables but poorly explains them or weakly links them to the scenario. (1–3 pts) Fails to mention correlation $\neq$ causation. No confounding variables identified.
Conclusion & Limitations
(6 pts)		 (5–6 pts) Strong, logical conclusion summarizing findings. Honestly identifies limits in data collection. (3–4 pts) Conclusion is present but lacks deep synthesis. Mentions limitations vaguely. (1–2 pts) Weak conclusion completely unrelated to the data. No limitations acknowledged.
